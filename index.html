Memory Reconstruction

Generate Images from a Story

Tell us a story or memory, and our AI will divide it into meaningful sections. For each section, the system searches for related artworks from our curated databases, helping you visualize and recall your memories through art.

How It Works

  1. Tell us a story or memory that you'd like to explore
  2. The AI divides your story into meaningful sections
  3. For each section, the system searches for related artworks
  4. Choose the image that best matches each section of your story

Example

The city towers glimmered above me like steel giants. I wandered toward the quiet outskirts where the noise softened. Sunlight spilled across open fields, turning everything warm and golden. Wildflowers brushed my legs as colors replaced concrete. And at last, a still blue lake opened before me.

About ArtEvoke

This thesis explores how artificial intelligence can support non-drug interventions for people living with Alzheimer's disease through the use of visual stimuli and art. We present the development and integration of ArtEvoke, a prototype platform that combines large multimodal models (LMMs), large language models (LLMs), and embedding-based vector search to connect personal stories with curated artworks.

Building upon foundational research from a Master's thesis by Augusto B. V. Silva and Marc Bejjani at Université Catholique de Louvain, which benchmarked the core AI models, this work focuses on integrating these components into a functional prototype and introducing a novel clinical evaluation framework. Designed to assist memory recall and cognitive engagement, ArtEvoke generates personalized visual narratives or retrieves relevant paintings based on the user's story. A key contribution of this thesis is the platform's ability to support structured clinical sessions, enabling practitioners to capture and "score" patient responses to provide a metric for engagement.

Research Goals

The prototype is intended for testing in future therapeutic and research settings as a scalable support tool. We evaluate the integrated system's retrieval effectiveness using FAISS and its user interaction design with a focus on accessibility. These findings highlight the potential of AI tools to support scalable and measurable cognitive stimulation in dementia care.

The Team

This work was undertaken at the University of São Paulo by Augusto B. V. Silva and Vinicius Alvarenga, in collaboration with researchers at Université Catholique de Louvain. The project represents a bridge between computer science, healthcare, and art history, demonstrating the interdisciplinary potential of AI in therapeutic contexts.

Technology Stack

ArtEvoke integrates several cutting-edge technologies:

  • Large Multimodal Models (LMMs) for understanding visual and textual content
  • Large Language Models (LLMs) for story generation and narrative creation
  • FAISS Vector Search for efficient similarity-based artwork retrieval
  • Embedding Models for semantic understanding of stories and artwork descriptions
  • Curated Art Databases including WikiArt, SemArt, and museum collections
